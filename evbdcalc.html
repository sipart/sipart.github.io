EV battery health estimator · version 3

With an added pinch of salt!

A non-linear EV battery degradation estimator with optimistic / typical / pessimistic bands, vehicle-family curves, gentler handling of brief 100% charging, passive-cooling penalties that depend on heat and rapid charging, and an option to account for buffer-masked usable capacity.

Non-linear ageing Models a quicker early drop, a flatter middle life, and optional late-life acceleration rather than age × flat percentage.
Likely range Shows a band because individual EVs vary wildly by climate, pack revision, BMS behaviour and hidden history.
Buffers matter Displayed 100% is often not cell-level 100%; some cars mask early cell ageing from driver-visible range.

1. Vehicle and battery

Starter profiles are approximate and editable. If you have LeafSpy, OBD, Aviloo, manufacturer app or service-menu SoH data, use the override.
For some vehicles with conservative or dynamic buffers, driver-visible range may stay healthier than raw cell degradation for several years.

Important disclaimer
This is an estimator, not a diagnostic. Real SoH needs BMS/OBD/app data or a battery health test. It gives a plausible range, not a guarantee, valuation, warranty opinion or engineering test result.
Version 3 model notes
Uses non-linear degradation, vehicle-family calibration, confidence bands, reduced penalties for brief 100% dwell, passive-cooling penalties tied to heat/DC use, and optional buffer masking for driver-visible usable capacity.
